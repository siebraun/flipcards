Yule and Winter Solstice Snacks

Fried Honeycakes

Beat one egg in a medium bowl. Combine 2/3 cup flour, 1/8 tsp cinnamon, 1/8 tsp salt & 2 tbsp sugar in a small bowl. Stir into the egg mixture. Let stand 30 minutes. Combine 1 cup of honey & 1/8 tsp nutmeg in a small bowl. Heat 1/2-inch of oil in a frying pan until hot, but not smoking. Drop the batter into the oil 1 tablespoon at a time frying until golden brown. Drain on paper towels. Dip into the honey and serve. (makes 1 1/2 doz.)

Celtic Yule Cakes

Beat 2 eggs, 1/2 cup butter, 1/2 tsp vanilla, orange zest and 2 cups sugar. Add 1 1/4 cup flour and 3/4 tsp baking powder. When well mixed add the tbsp of milk. Pour into a well floured/greased cup cake tin and bake in an oven preheated to 375 degrees F for 20 to 25 minutes. In a small bowl blend 2-3 tbsp boiling water and 2 cups powdered sugar to make the icing. Lace over celtic cakes before serving.

Moon Cookies

Cream together 1 cup butter and 1 1/4 cup sugar until fluffy and light. Add grated lemon peel, 1/4 tsp salt, 1 1/3 cup flour,1 1/2 cup grated almonts, and 1 tsp. vanilla; mix thoroughly. Cover and chill thoroughly. When the dough is well chilled, roll out dough to and cut with moon/crescent cookie cutter. Bake in preheated 375Â° oven for 8-10 minutes.

Dust powdered confectioners sugar over warm cookies and let cool.

Peppermint Fudge

Place 16 oz semi-sweet baker's chocolate and 1 tbsp butter in a microwave-safe bowl. Warm in the microwave until it begins to soften. Once melted, stir together until well blended. Add 14 oz sweetened, condensed milk, and mix well. Add 2-3 drops of peppermint extract. Line 8 x 8-inch pan with foil and lightly butter. Spread fudge mixture into pan evenly. Refrigerate until cool. Once hardened, remove from the pan, and cut into pieces.

Sun King Soup

Saute 1 small diced onion in 3 tbs butter over low heat. Chop 2 1/2 cups tomatoes and add to the onions. Allow to cook until tomatoes become soft, just a few minutes. Add 1 cup broth, and allow the whole thing to simmer over low heat for 30 minutes.

Transfer entire mixture into a blender and puree, adding 3/4 cup orange juice. Return to stovetop, add rosemary, and simmer for 5 - 10 mins. Add paprika to taste. Serve in warm bowls with soft bread.
